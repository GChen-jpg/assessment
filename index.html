Reference Guide

Assessment in Online
Radiation Protection Safety

What great assessment looks like, sounds like, and feels like for adult learners in an online radiation protection safety program - with an interactive mind map companion.

Garielle Chen  |  Online Learning and Instructional Design
Radiation protection safety training has long relied on compliance-based assessment that confirms a learner has completed a module rather than confirming they can make a sound decision in a real radiation environment. This reference guide argues for a different approach grounded in adult learning theory, behaviour change research, and the specific cognitive demands of radiation protection work. The interactive mind map below maps this framework across three dimensions: what assessment looks like, sounds like, and feels like for learners in an online program.
Looks Like

Visual Evidence of Learning

Assessment that makes learning visible through what the learner produces or interacts with.

  • Scenario-based decision tasks with radiation work area diagrams
  • Interactive dose calculation exercises
  • Labelled zone identification tasks
  • Dosimetry reading simulations
  • Progress dashboard across regulatory topics
Sounds Like

Effective Feedback Language

Assessment feedback that teaches rather than merely evaluates - specific, plain, and grounded in real-world consequence.

  • Explains reasoning behind dose limits, not just the number
  • Distinguishes public vs. occupational exposure contexts
  • Connects ALARA to professional obligation
  • Addresses internal vs. external exposure pathways
  • Suggests a concrete next step after every error
Feels Like

The Learner's Experience

Assessment that builds confidence, respects experience, and feels like preparation rather than evaluation.

  • Relevant to the learner's actual job role and facility
  • Consequential but not punitive
  • Builds competence, not just compliance
  • Respects prior workplace experience
  • Self-directed and revisable
